Ensemble Scores

Humming

Make a quiet sound that is soothing.

Listen more than you play.

Make another sound that is soothing.

Continue listening. Continue contributing sounds.

...

Continue this process until you hear a sound which is particularly satisfying to you.

When you hear it, stop playing and hum a comfortable tone for the duration of a long exhale.

When you hear anyone begin to hum, stop playing and hum a [different] comfortable tone for the duration of a long exhale.

At the end of the breath, wait for everyone to stop humming and sit in the resultant silence for an extended moment.

...

Repeat this cycle

Meditation

Anyone

initiates by playing a long tone the length of a normal exhale.

Another

person plays a long tone so that

Two

people are holding a tone together.

Pause

after each tone.

Allow

someone new to initiate every time.

Maintain

this as long as possible.

...

If more than one person enters, the rules of the meditation change.

Anyone

initiates by playing a long tone the length of a normal exhale.

Two

other people play a long tone so that

Three

people are holding a tone together.

Pause

after each tone.

Allow

someone new to initiate every time.

Maintain

this as long as possible.

...

If more than two people enter, the rules of the meditation change.

Anyone

initiates by playing a long tone the length of a normal exhale.

Three

other people play a long tone so that

Four

people are holding a tone together.

Pause

after each tone.

Allow

someone new to initiate every time.

Maintain

this as long as possible.

...

If more than three people enter, the rules of the meditation change.

Continue this process until the entire ensemble is joining the initiated tone together.

Maintain until no one initiates.

Inhale, Exhale

Perform this piece for as long as is safe.

Sit huddled in a circle, heads touching.

Hold a tone, shift the pitch slightly for every entrance.

One at time, clockwise, breathe loudly through pursed lips.

Before breathing:

Taper off tone so that the entrance goes unnoticed.

After breathing:

Enter immediately with a tapered tone so that the entrance goes unnoticed.
