Prompt Scores

Notes

Examination of performative and compositional ritual.

Recognition of imposing vestigial western musical conventions.

Removal:

Intention, expectation, completion

Notation of rhythm, pitch, form

Requisite for validity resulting from labor

Structure and formality

Desire - Cultivation of a performative experience involving:

Manufacture of creative impulse

Observation of self and others

Reinterpretation of borrowed elements:

Graphic scores as visual prompts

Text score as a codification of process

Amorphous quality as a conduit to creative freedom.

Resultant survey of universality of creative impulse and facilitation of a shared experience.

Version 1 (ensemble)

Set up a table with graphic scores. Feel free to sit at the table at any point in order to participate in the following:

Someone will select a graphic score from a pile. They will set it on top, and whoever wants to participate will join them.

The person who selected the score will use numerical fingers to assign an order for the following process.

1st player

Look at the image.

Play an idea based on the creative impulse generated by the image.

This idea should be conceived of as a suggestion and not developed by the performer.

Stop and listen to the next player.

...

2nd player

Do not overlap with the previous player.

Play your own ‚Äúsuggestion‚Äù based on the creative impulse generated by the image.

Stop and listen to the next player.

...

Continue until all players have played their idea; collectively pause for a short moment.

Using the sounded motives as a starting point, improvise as much or as little as you want.

Version 2 (solo)

Surround yourself with the graphic prompts.

Create a situation where you can have a long period of time to process stream of consciousness.

At your own disgretion, relate your ideas to the images.

As you seek new ideas, find inspiration in the surrounding images.
