Humming

Listen to your surroundings. This is the main action of the participant.

Contribute a quiet sound that is soothing and relaxing to produce.

The total length of a sound should not exceed 5 seconds.

Listen more than you play.

Make another contribution.

Continue listening. Continue contributing sounds.

...

Continue this process until you hear a sound which produces a chill in the body.

When you feel it, stop playing and hum a comfortable tone for the duration of a comfortably long exhale.

Hum based soley on what is physically comfortable, without considering pitch.

When you finish, wait for everyone to stop humming and sit in the resulting silence for an extended moment.

...

Repeat this cycle for any length of time.

Inhale, Exhale

Sit huddled in a circle.

The group begins by producing a held soft tone

based soley on what is physically comfortable,

without considerating pitch.

Clockwise, one at a time, each person completes the following three tasks:

(1) Taper off the tone so that the decay goes unnoticed, then immediately

(2) inhale sharply and loudly through pursed lips, then immediately

(3) produce a tapered tone so that the entrance goes unnoticed.

Shift the pitch slightly for every entrance.

The result should be a held chord and a pulse of breath which acts as a metronome.

...

Perform this piece for as long as is safe.

The pacing of breathing will determine the size of the circle.

If the circle is too large, there will be too much time between breaths.

If the circle is too small, the entrances and exits will be too noticable.

This piece can be performed by a large ensemble if it is divided into several small groups.
