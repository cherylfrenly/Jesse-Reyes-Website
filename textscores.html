Text Scores

Humming

Make a quiet sound that is soothing.

The total length of a sound should not exceed 5 seconds.

Listen more than you play.

Make another sound that is soothing.

Continue listening. Continue contributing sounds.

...

Continue this process until you hear a sound which produces a chill in the body

When you hear it, stop playing and hum a comfortable tone for the duration of a long exhale.

When you hear anyone begin to hum, stop playing and hum a [different] comfortable tone for the duration of a long exhale.

At the end of the breath, wait for everyone to stop humming and sit in the resulting silence for an extended moment.

...

Repeat this cycle for any length of time.

Meditation

initiates by producing a long tone the length of a comfortably long exhale.

Another

Two

Pause

after each tone.

Allow

Maintain

this as long as possible.

Anyone

Continue this process until the entire ensemble is joining the initiated tone together.

...

Maintain until no one initiates.

Inhale, Exhale

Sit huddled in a circle, heads touching.

The group begins by producing a comfortable and soft tone.

Clockwise, one at time:

Taper off the tone so that the entrance goes unnoticed.

Inhale sharply and loudly through pursed lips.

Enter immediately with a tapered tone so that the entrance goes unnoticed.

Shift the pitch slightly for every entrance.

...

Perform this piece for as long as is safe.

The pacing of breathing will determine the size of the circle.

If the circle is too large, there will be too much time between breaths.

If the circle is too small, the entrances and exits will be too noticable.

This piece can be performed by a large ensemble if it is divided into several small groups.
